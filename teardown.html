Teardown: Oscillating Multi-Tool

As part of a project for Engineering Design, I fully disassembled and analyzed a Hammerhead oscillating multi-tool -- the kind you'd find in a DIY home toolbox.

This involved documenting its user-centered design, testing first use, breaking it down into over 20 individual components, and then CADing it all together. The internals were simpler than expected -- but the outer shell, with all its subtle geometry, was definitely a pain.

Still, tearing it apart (and putting it back together) gave us a clear look at how thoughtful, and sometimes frustrating, everyday product design can be.

The Process

After observing the product, the first step was to take measurements of its dimensions.

Then we took the whole thing apart and labeled each of the components.

Following a full product decomposition, I recreated the components in SolidWorks, and then assembled them all together.
