Luke's Lightsaber Replica

As part of a three-person team, I created a replica of Luke Skywalker's lightsaber hilt, accompanied by a laser-cut display stand. It involved a lot of CAD modeling on SolidWorks, sheet metal bending, machining, sand casting, and calculations for material forming.

Our initial plan was to just make the hilt, but once we got going, we knew we had to bring the whole thing to life. We ordered a lightsaber electronics kit that we installed inside the hilt, along with a polycarbonate tube for the blade.

From converting CAD drawings into tangible components to resolving practical issues like material limitations, this project taught me a lot about turning an idea into something real — and how much problem solving happens along the way.

Machining was a super time consuming process. All of the holes had to be precisely drilled and the shroud at the end of the hilt had to be shaped. We found that the easiest way to do this was by printing out a template from the Replica Prop Forum and alligning it with the hilt. This template had markings for holes and the shroud shape, and our additional templates were made by hand.
